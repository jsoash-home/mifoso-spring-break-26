Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

1 Tram Way · Adults ~$41 · kids ~$27.50 (confirm current rates)

Book ahead — spring break crowds are real. World's largest rotating tram car; ~10 min to 8,516 ft. Pack layers — often 30–40°F cooler on top. 50+ miles of trails up there.

⛳ Golf near Mission Hills

Rough guide for Westin Mission Hills Resort Villas (Rancho Mirage / Palm Desert) — resort courses, public tracks, and desert golf reality checks. Green fees swing wildly by season, day, and tee time; always confirm on the course website or pro shop before you book.

Desert golf tips: Book early — spring break weeks fill. Bring extra balls (wind & water); carry water even on a cart; first-tee times beat peak heat. Collared shirts & soft spikes are the usual dress-code baseline; flip-flops are a no-go at most clubs.

🏀 Pickup & public courts

Where to get shots up in the Coachella Valley — mostly outdoor public parks (free or low-cost), plus gym options if you want indoor hoops. Hours, pickup traffic, and net quality vary — bring water & a ball.

Reality check: March afternoons are hot. Courts near playgrounds & schools get busy after 4pm. Be cool: call "next" on busy runs, share the court, and don't leave valuables in the car.

Indoor & gym hoops

  • Desert Recreation District (DRD) — runs community centers & gyms across the valley. Some locations offer open gym / basketball on a schedule (sometimes small drop-in fee vs membership). Start here: myrecreationdistrict.com — call the center nearest your hotel for March hours.
  • YMCA of the Desert — branches in Palm Springs, Desert Hot Springs, etc. — often have indoor courts for members; ask about guest / day passes. ymcaofthedesert.org
  • Commercial gyms (LA Fitness, 24 Hour Fitness, etc.) — usually have indoor hoops for members; bring a friend with a guest pass if you have one.

Court conditions & pickup culture change — always verify nets, lights, and hours. Not affiliated with any park or gym.
